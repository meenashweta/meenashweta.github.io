Computational & Molecular Engineering Lab

NIT Kurukshetra

Computational & Molecular Engineering (CME) Lab is passionate about engineering various 2D materials and their computational approaches for spintronics, optoelectronics and energy related applications. Our aim is to translate creative ideas into impactful outcomes. Our group members are well trained not only in basic science and research, but also in applied engineering leading to top tier projects. Our group is focusing on Academia-Industry translational bridge. We work as a team and believe in grooming raw talent in fulfilling their goals and attaining success as a unit with expertise from various fields. This is a write place for independent researcher exploring innovative ideas with great fun.


SHWETA MEENA

Dr. Shweta Meena is currently working as an Assistant Professor at National Institute of Technology, Kurukshetra, India

Dr. Shweta completed her Bachelor of Technology in Electronics & Communication Engineering from JNTU Hyderabad, India in 2009 with distinction. She subsequently went on to finish a Master of Technology in VLSI System Design and a Ph.D. in Graphene based Magnetic Tunnel Junctions for Spintronics applications from JNTU, Hyderabad and National Institute of Technology, Kurukshetra, India, respectively.

From 2019, Dr. Shweta has started independent research group in the area of computation of 2D materials and their molecular engineering. Her group’s main focus is to corroborate computational approaches for optoelectronic, spintronic and energy-related applications of 2D materials such as MXenes, Black Phosphorus, Boron Nitride, Graphene and CNTs. Her research works have been published and highlighted by top tier journals like The Journal of Physical Chemistry, Physical Chemistry Chemical physics, Physics Letters A, ChemistrySelect, RSC Advances, Journal of Physics: Condensed Matter, Chemical Physics Letters, etc

Dr. Shweta was offered a visiting faculty position at Tufts University, Medford, Boston, USA.
